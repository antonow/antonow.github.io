What is pair programming?

Pair programming is the process of working with another person to complete a challenge. The advantages of pair programming include: both people being able to learn from eachother and having multiple perspectives makes troubleshooting much easier. Also, working with others better prepares you for a real work environment in which you are expected to work with coworkers and clients.

Why is pair programming important to DBC?

Because of DBC's focus on emotional intelligence, pair programming is important in developing the abilty to communicate and understand eachothers needs.

DBC also has a strong focus in developing programmers that are part of a community, and that is why participating in online discussions and working in pairs in encouraged.

Compare Driver and Navigator

Driver: The person at the keyboard that is primarily in charge of translating the pair's ideas into working code.

Navigator: The person in the group that dictates the ideas and helps guide the driver.

While there are two separete roles, the two work together and help with each task. They are both equally important in completeing the project.
